III · The Method

A systematic framework.

Al-Hikmah — "the wisdom" — is a discipline, not a hunch. A simple, quantitative framework designed to outperform the S&P 500 and other benchmark ETFs.

i.

Quantitative screen

Every quarter, the full S&P 500 universe is run through a rules-based screen that ranks each constituent on a proprietary set of factors. The five strongest signals form the next quarter's equity allocation — no narrative, no discretion.

ii.

Risk-balanced allocation

Selections are deployed as 60% of capital, with 30% in physical gold and 10% in cash. The gold sleeve cushions equity drawdowns; the cash sleeve provides dry powder. The result: meaningfully lower volatility than pure equity exposure.

iii.

Disciplined rebalancing

Every position is closed at quarter end. Capital flows back into the next five selections, rebalanced to target weights. The same rules applied for nearly three decades — through tech bubbles, financial crises, and COVID alike.

V · Questions

Asked & answered.

How are selections delivered?
The five quarterly selections arrive by email on the first trading day of every quarter — January, April, July, and October. Each delivery includes the tickers, allocation weights, the previous quarter's results, and a written summary. All selections are archived in your subscriber dashboard.
What is the investment universe?
The full S&P 500. Every quarter we identify the five constituents that best satisfy our quantitative criteria and deploy 60% of capital across them equal-weighted. The remaining 40% sits in a defensive sleeve of gold (30%) and cash (10%) to manage drawdowns.
How does the strategy compare to other ETFs?
Over the documented 29-year period, the strategy has compounded at 31.3% annually versus 17.3% for the S&P 500 (SPY) — a 14 percentage point annualised alpha. The defensive sleeve also produces a meaningfully better Sharpe ratio than a pure equity benchmark, and outperforms low-volatility ETFs like USMV and SPLV on a risk-adjusted basis.
Can I cancel any time?
Yes. Monthly subscribers can cancel at any time and retain access through the end of the billing period. Annual subscribers are protected by a 30-day money-back guarantee — full refund, no questions asked.
How is this different from Seeking Alpha or Motley Fool?
We publish one allocation a quarter — not twenty calls a month. There is no community forum, no upsell ladder, no flashing dashboards. You receive a disciplined, rules-based strategy with nearly three decades of performance history, delivered with clarity and brevity.
